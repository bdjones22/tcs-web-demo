Clinician Profile

Akeya Watley, LPCC, LADC

Direct, down-to-earth, and culturally responsive care for anxiety, depression, trauma, substance use, and major life transitions.

Specialties: Anxiety · Depression · Trauma · Substance Use · Life Transitions
Populations: Adults and families · Multicultural and BIPOC communities

Life's journey comes with its share of burdens—anxiety, depression, past trauma, and difficult transitions. My name is Akeya Watley, and as an LPCC and LADC, I provide a compassionate, straightforward, and culturally responsive space for you to navigate these challenges. My mission is to guide you toward healing, empowering you to live a healthy, authentic, and happy life.

A direct, down-to-earth therapeutic style

My therapeutic approach is warm, down-to-earth, and always centered on your unique needs. I'm known for blending a direct approach with a good sense of humor, which helps clients feel at ease as we work to uncover what truly matters. Therapy with me is a space where you can be honest, feel deeply supported, and make real progress—and if we share a laugh along the way, that's just part of the healing process.

Culturally competent and trauma-informed care

As an African American female psychotherapist, I am acutely aware of the unique social and emotional landscapes many of my clients navigate. I am committed to creating a space where you feel seen, heard, and understood without judgment, while honoring the cultural context of your experiences. My practice is also trauma-informed, ensuring a safe and supportive environment for those seeking to process and heal from the emotional and social impacts of trauma.

Empowering your journey with evidence-based practices

To support your growth, I utilize a range of evidence-based practices and interventions, tailored to your needs and goals:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): To help identify and shift unhelpful thought patterns.
  • Mindfulness: Building awareness and grounding in the present moment.
  • Trauma-Informed Therapy: Processing and integrating the lasting effects of trauma with care and safety.
  • Substance Use Counseling (LADC): Supporting individuals navigating addiction, recovery, and co-occurring concerns.

Your courage to thrive

Whether you are seeking to manage anxiety, overcome depression, heal from trauma, or navigate a major life transition, I am here to support you. Therapy with me is about making meaningful progress, rediscovering your strength, and honoring your right to a healthy and joyful life.
